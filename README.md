## Responses to all the possible objections any human being can ever possibly think of

Here is a comprehensive FAQ of answers to questions by humans who think I'm simply depressed and unable to see the 'beauty of life' for me to have built an extremely dark worldview and not something that I came to through years of learning science and intellectual honesty, and grappling with the true scale of horror that is life on earth. Rather we should build silly distractions to keep us occupied and reduce suffering in the meantime through the labour of researchers and scientists who themselves never asked to be born and will not get to enjoy the fruits of their labour and will nevertheless have children so that at least someone gets to enjoy that utopian future and would rather NOT want extinction to happen so that they get to feel super important that their work could have some use afterall and was not a complete and utter waste since it all eventually burns down and ends up in a thermal equilibrium wasteland instead?

Ans: While instantaneous annihilation might seem like the most effective way to eliminate suffering, nuclear weapons are fundamentally weapons of unimaginable terror and violence. A nuclear holocaust would not be instant for everyone; millions would survive the initial blasts only to suffer through excrutiating radiation poisoning, starvation in a nuclear winter, and the violent collapse of civilization. This would create an unprecedented spike in pure agony. The core philosophy of PPEP is a _peaceful_ and _graceful_ exit. A violent, chaotic end completely contradicts the goal of minimizing pain. We must engineer a solution that gently winds down the existential machine, akin to placing a patient under general anesthesia from which they peacefully never wake.

- Q2: What if we can just fix the world? Instead of extinction, why don't we build a utopia, solve poverty, cure diseases, and eliminate suffering through technology and social progress?

Ans: A "utopia" is a moving target that can never eliminate the fundamental biological and psychological scaffolding of suffering. Even if we miraculously cure all physical diseases and ensure infinite resources, the human mind is engineered by evolution to be perpetually dissatisfied in order to drive survival. Furthermore, achieving this hypothetical future utopia requires the guaranteed, non-consensual suffering of billions of sentient beings in the present and near future. It is a profound moral failure to force current and future generations into the meat grinder of existence as sacrificial stepping stones for a speculative, practically unattainable paradise that they will never get to experience.

- Q3: But what about the joy, love, art, and beauty in the world? Aren't those worth the suffering? Don't they give existence value?

Ans: The presence of pleasure in some privileged lives **does not justify** or mathematically cancel out the excruciating, unmitigated agony experienced by those in the margins. The capacity to experience joy, love, and beauty mostly exist as a temporary relief mechanism from a baseline state of constant need and deprivation. You only enjoy eating because you experience the discomfort of hunger; you only seek love because you suffer from the painful void of isolation. Positive experiences are merely the fleeting alleviation of negative states. Imposing life is a gamble where you risk someone else's unimaginable suffering for the sake of their potential joy—a severe violation of their consent. No amount of great art can justify the torture of a single innocent being. Parents have sex for a few minutes of fun, and end up sentencing their child to a lifetime of suffering.

Ans: This is the most common and **intellectually lazy objection**. The PPEP is not born out of personal despair, but from a profound, agonizing **empathy** for _all_ sentient life. Individual suicide does not stop the machinery of suffering; it leaves trillions of other beings—human and non-human—trapped in the relentless cycle of predation, disease, and exploitation. The fact that is see all this _so_ clearly gives me **more** of a reason to stay alive, to gain more power and influence to be able to achieve this without having to convince retards like you who ask these questions. A solitary exit is merely a tragic failure of the individual to cope; a coordinated, peaceful, universal exit is a triumph of ultimate compassion. The goal is not to escape the slaughterhouse alone while leaving the doors locked behind you, but to gracefully dismantle the slaughterhouse entirely so no one else has to bleed.

- Q5: HOW do you plan to achieve this?! This is impossible, so therefore not worth attempting, and you may end up causing MORE suffering in the process!

Ans: It might seem like an insurmountable technological hurdle today, but human ingenuity has split the atom, mapped the genome, and engineered synthetic life. But the **guaranteed, infinite suffering** of indefinite future generations mathematically dwarfs any temporary, finite suffering that _might_ occur during a botched execution. The risk of doing nothing is **absolute certainty of endless torture for trillions upon trillions of beings**. Attempting to stop the machine is the only morally defensible action, even with a margin of error.

PPEP doesn't fulfill your impossible material desires—it eliminates the humiliating *need* and *craving* for them in the first place. You skip the grinding labor camp entirely and are returned to the unburdened void, the only state of true, infinite peace.
