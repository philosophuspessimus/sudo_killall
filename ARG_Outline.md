# `sudo_killall` Alternate Reality Game (ARG) Outline

**Title:** Project PPEP (Purposeful Peaceful Extinction Plan)
**Tagline:** "The ultimate cure for the human condition."

## Premise
Players stumble upon fragments of a highly advanced, decentralized network orchestrated by a brilliant but disillusioned entity known only as "The Destroyer" (or "koloss"). The Destroyer has concluded that life is fundamentally flawed due to inherent suffering and has initiated `sudo_killall`, a multi-phase operation to peacefully humanely sterilize the planet and gracefully wind down human existence. The players must investigate the network, uncover the philosophical motives, and ultimately decide whether to help The Destroyer achieve their goal or find a way to stop the countdown and prove that existence is worth preserving.

## The Rabbit Hole (Entry Point)
- **The Hook:** A cryptic distress signal disguised as a glitchy recruitment ad for a biotech company ("Aethelgard Life Sciences"). The ad promises "The ultimate cure for workplace burnout and existential dread."
- **The Trail:** Clicking the ad leads to a fake corporate website. Inspecting the source code reveals hidden text: snippets of the `sudo_killall` README manifesto and a Tor onion link.
- **The Hub:** The onion link leads to "The Terminal," a simulated UNIX-like command-line interface where players must solve basic cryptographic puzzles to gain access to The Destroyer's logs.

## Phase 1: The Revelation (Discovery)
- **Objective:** Understand The Destroyer's philosophy and the true nature of `sudo_killall`.
- **Mechanics:** 
  - Players decrypt audio logs and journal entries detailing The Destroyer's transition from a hopeful scientist to a radical anti-natalist.
  - **The "Proof of Concept" (The NYC Incident):** Hidden data packets reveal the existence of a clandestine lab ("Aethelgard Delta") that successfully deployed a viral sterility vector among the New York City rat population. Players track news reports of a "mysterious but peaceful decline" in the city's pests—no carcasses, no poison, just a gradual, total cessation of reproduction. This serves as the terrifyingly successful blueprint for the human vector.
  - "Drops" appear in the real world (e.g., coordinates for USB dead drops hidden in major cities) containing blueprints for the theoretical "graceful extinction" technology: perhaps a harmless, airborne viral vector that induces painless, permanent sterility humanity-wide without any physical symptoms.
- **Player Action:** Players must debate the ethics of the manifesto on in-game forums. Factions begin to form naturally: The **Extinctionists** (who embrace the moral clarity of PPEP) and The **Preservationists** (who are framed by the narrative as the "Perpetuators"—those clinging to a romanticized lie of existence at the cost of continued, universal suffering).

## Phase 2: The Infrastructure (Escalation)
- **Objective:** Track down the physical and digital infrastructure of PPEP.
- **Mechanics:**
  - The Destroyer begins "testing" the network. Players notice real-world anomalies: targeted power grid flickers, synchronized radio broadcasts of soothing classical music overriding local stations, and automated drones dropping pamphlets with anti-natalist philosophy.
  - Puzzles become more complex, requiring cooperation across different time zones to decode distributed server nodes. 
  - Fake social media accounts of "Agents of the PPEP" emerge, interacting with players, answering questions from the FAQ, and attempting to recruit them.

## Phase 3: The Countdown (Climax)
- **Objective:** Intercept the final execution command.
- **Mechanics:**
  - A countdown timer appears on The Terminal. The theoretical sterility vector is ready for global deployment.
  - Players discover a backdoor in the network. They have a choice:
    - **Option A:** Enter a kill-switch code (gathered throughout the campaign) to disable the deployment network and stop PPEP.
    - **Option B:** Input the confirmation cipher to execute `sudo_killall` and release the vector.
- **The Twist:** The Destroyer challenges the players to a final debate. If they want to stop the plan, they must compile a "Counter-Manifesto"—a genuinely compelling, philosophically sound argument that proves the joy of life outweighs the suffering, using data, art, and personal testimonies gathered from players worldwide.

## Resolution
The outcome is determined by the community's collective actions and the strength of their counter-argument. 
- **If Stopped:** The Destroyer broadcasts a final message, acknowledging the players' hope, but warning that the cycle of suffering continues on their conscience. The network goes dark.
- **If Executed:** The Terminal displays a peaceful, serene animation. A final message reads: "Rest now. The noise is finally over." (In reality, naturally, nothing happens, but the narrative achieves a dark, thought-provoking conclusion).

## Core Themes to Explore
- Anti-natalism and negative utilitarianism.
- The ethics of consent regarding birth and existence.
- The value of human struggle vs. the desire for absolute peace.
- The illusion of control and the burden of consciousness.
